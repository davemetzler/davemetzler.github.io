  •  About the GIS stuff

This map represents the integration of several much smarter peoples tools which include Bootstrap 3, MapBox, Leaflet, OSMBuildings, and the work of Brian McBride. I have taken some static maps off of the Denver Public Library’s website, gave them the good old GIS treatment and brought them to you!

Data
  • The Sanborn Maps come from The DPL public map collection
  • The Buildings are courtesy of OSM Buildings
  • You can get imagery anywhere but this fine example is from ESRI.
  • Modern roads from the fine folks at GeoIQ.

This is all here in good fun.

I have made every attempt to utilize best georeferencing practices but the original files were a bit of a mess to work with. Consequently some of the blocks are a little off but I think everyone gets the idea! It was a bit of a process to get the images and stitch them together which is why they are incomplete but still, History!

  • Sanborn maps were created primarily to assess fire risk and they contain a wealth of knowledge.
  • Since I am at my core incredibly cheap I am only allotted a certain amount of storage for the sanborn maps. I picked the CBD to show off all of the neat stuff you can do with these tools.
  • The buildings here are from an open source Dataset so while they are quite good not everything has a name therefore the address is substituted. If that information wasn’t information it is null right now.
  • I just grabbed a couple of tools to show off leaflet but there are countless more that could be in here.
